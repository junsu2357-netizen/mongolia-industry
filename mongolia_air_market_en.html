01 — Crisis Overview

How Bad Is
Mongolia's Air?

🚨 Public Health Emergency

During heating season, Ulaanbaatar's PM2.5 levels reach among the highest in the world. UNICEF has warned that children in Ulaanbaatar face significantly higher rates of lower respiratory infections than those in rural areas. Mongolia ranks among the world's highest-impact countries for air pollution-related mortality.

02 — Emission Sources

What's Polluting
The Air?

In 2017, Mongolia emitted 10 kt of black carbon, 121.5 kt of PM2.5, and 121.9 kt of NOx. Coal combustion for heating and freight transport are the dominant drivers.

🏠 Household Sector — #1 Polluter
Coal burning in Ulaanbaatar's ger districts dominates BC, PM2.5, and CO emissions. UB alone accounts for roughly half of all household-sector emissions nationally.
🚛 Transport — NOx Driver
Freight transport produces over 90% of transport-sector BC, PM2.5, and NOx. As of 2018, Euro 5 fuel accounted for just 7.7% of the total fuel mix.
⚡ Power & Heat Generation
CHP4 alone is responsible for ~35% of all electricity and heat sector emissions. All plants run on coal, emitting 9,883 kt of CO₂ annually.

04 — Market Opportunity

6 Reasons To Enter
Mongolia Now

01
Extreme Pollution = Extreme Demand
Winter PM2.5 levels routinely exceed WHO guidelines by dozens of times. The pain point is visceral and undeniable — demand for indoor air quality solutions is immediate and high-urgency.
02
Policy Tailwinds
NDC and Air Quality Strategy explicitly target household air quality improvement. Government procurement programs and subsidy schemes offer a viable B2G entry channel.
03
Children & Vulnerable Populations
Post-UNICEF warnings, parental anxiety about children's respiratory health is extremely high. Schools, kindergartens, and hospitals represent a fast-growing institutional B2B segment.
04
Ger Districts — Blue Ocean
200,000+ ger households actively burning coal for heat. Government electrification drive creates a natural bundle opportunity. A low-power, compact product line could capture this underserved segment.
05
Growing Middle Class
Mining-driven GDP growth is expanding Ulaanbaatar's middle class. Health awareness is rising rapidly, and willingness-to-pay for premium products is following. Korean brands carry strong trust equity.
06
Competitive White Space
The premium segment is largely uncontested beyond Chinese low-cost imports. First-mover advantage is available. A credible, well-positioned brand can own this category before it matures.
05 — Entry Strategy

The Case For
Entering Now

Mongolia Is A Market That Can't Wait

Mongolia's air pollution is not an environmental footnote — it is a full-scale public health crisis. A mortality rate of 132 per 100,000 (43% above the global mean), with 29–40% of cardiopulmonary and lung cancer deaths attributable to air pollution, signals a market where the pain is acute and the willingness to act is high.

Government policy is already moving — NDC targets, ger district electrification, vehicle emission upgrades. This transition period is the golden window: pollution won't be fixed overnight, but purchasing power and health awareness are rising fast. The market will be won before the pollution is solved.

1
B2B First: Schools, hospitals, corporate offices in Ulaanbaatar. Low acquisition cost, high-volume procurement, reference credibility builder.
2
D2C Consumer: Premium household units targeting Ulaanbaatar's middle class. Lead with health narrative and child safety messaging.
3
Ger District: Affordable, compact product line tied to government electrification subsidies. High volume, high social impact, strong PR story.
